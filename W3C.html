W3C

The W3C, or World Wide Web Consortium, is an “international community that develops open standards to ensure the long-term growth of the Web” (W3C). It was created in 1994 by Tim Berners-Lee, and is intended to improve the web as a whole. Definition adapted from “About W3C.” W3C, 1 Oct. 1994, www.w3.org/. by Madeline Harrant
